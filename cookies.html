Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 12, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used by website owners to make their sites work more efficiently and provide reporting information.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, WinnerCanadaMaple) are called "first-party cookies". Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies". Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality on or through the website (e.g., advertising, interactive content, and analytics).

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use first-party and third-party cookies for several reasons:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for platform functionality and security
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings
  • Performance Cookies: Analyze platform performance
  • Analytics Cookies: Understand how visitors use our platform
  • Advertising Cookies: Display relevant advertisements

4. Other Tracking Technologies

In addition to cookies, we also use other tracking technologies:

4.1 Web Beacons

Web beacons are small graphic images embedded in web pages and emails. They are used to track email opens and content interaction.

4.2 Tracking Pixels

Tracking pixels are code fragments that allow us to collect information about visitors, such as browser type, operating system, and visit time.

4.3 Local Storage

We use browser local storage to save certain data on your device for improved performance and user experience.

6. Managing Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. You can manage your cookie preferences in several ways:

6.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can configure your browser to:

  • Block all cookies
  • Allow only first-party cookies
  • Delete cookies when closing the browser
  • Receive notifications before cookies are set

6.2 Cookie Management in Popular Browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings ‚Üí Privacy and security ‚Üí Cookies
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Cookies
  • Opera: Settings ‚Üí Advanced ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Cookies

7. Consequences of Disabling Cookies

It's important to understand that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our platform:

  • Essential cookies: Disabling will cause core platform features to malfunction
  • Functional cookies: You will lose personalization and saved settings
  • Analytics cookies: We won't be able to improve our platform based on usage data
